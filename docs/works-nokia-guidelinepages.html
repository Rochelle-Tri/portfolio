Nokia Freeform Design System

UX/UI Designer Co-op || UX/UI Design, Mockuping, Component Research

Team

Cloud Networking Service

Timeline

Sept - Dec 2021

Tools

Sketch, Abstract, Jira

The Newest Design System

Our team’s primary focus was to build an all encompassing design and component library. This internal tool would replace the existing text-heavy design manual and house comprehensive guidelines, usage instructions, and visual examples to support Nokia’s designers and developers across teams. The library was designed using Storybook , an open-source UI development environment that aimed to centralize knowledge and streamline the adoption of Nokia’s design standards.

Key Responsibilities

I was responsible for researching, ideating, wireframing, and designing mockups for component guideline pages within Storybook. These pages covered essential design concepts such as localization, iconography, and empty states, reflecting Nokia’s branding while establishing accessible design standards.

Additionally, I was tasked with designing a dialog modal that would appear in the Design System to indicate the technical “health” status of a page (e.g., errors, warnings, or loading issues). The purpose of the dialog modal was to notify developers which pages in the system had issues to deal with. After finalizing the dialog design, I integrated it into a high-fidelity prototype of the library using Marvel, which was then presented to cross-functional teams across departments.

Understanding The System and Researching Components

With every different topic, I started by reviewing Nokia’s internal design manuals and exploring external systems like Adobe Spectrum and Shopify’s Polaris React. to understand how other organizations structure their design documentation. Once I had a solid grasp of the required topics, I conducted further research to define key concepts, best practices, and common mistakes for each guideline. Using this information, I sketched layout ideas and determined how to best organize the content visually.

Revising

I created high-fidelity mockups in Sketch and uploaded them to Abstract for team feedback. Each iteration was shaped by regular critique sessions, where I revised both the content and layout based on insights from my peers. Once a design was finalized, I handed it off to developers through Jira, completing the sprint cycle.

Re-envisioning the Dialog Modal

For the dialog modal, I researched how other design systems communicate technical issues and interface health. I explored existing modal patterns and examined how similar dialogs were structured in terms of content and visual hierarchy. Researching and working with a topic I knew nothing of was a challenge that allowed me to further my understanding of UX design outside of what I learned in school.

Overcoming the Unknown

The onboarding phase was steep, since I had to quickly familiarize myself with new platforms, Nokia’s design language, and unfamiliar UX vocabulary as a student with very little existing UX/UI knowledge. One of the toughest tasks was designing the Localization guideline, which had limited documentation. I completed the mockup by proactively reaching out to designers and developers across teams for deeper insights.

Reflection

My first co-op taught me the importance of adaptability, proactive learning, and cross-team communication. I gained a solid understanding of design system thinking and strengthened my skills in creating documentation that balances visual clarity with technical detail.

While designing the dialog modal, I explored and learned about components like tabs, tooltips, and scrollbars while ideating my different drafts. This experience was valuable even if the drafts with those components never made it to the final design.

I also learned to pace my workflow—rushing to meet sprint deadlines led to avoidable errors. In the future, I’d build in time to thoroughly review my work before requesting peer feedback, ensuring more efficient collaboration.
