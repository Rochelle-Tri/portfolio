"Design, if it is to be ecologically responsible and socially responsive, must be revolutionary and radical." — Victor Papanek

Hi, I’m Rochelle (they/them)! I’m a Canadian-born Vietnamese queer designer with an interest in UX research and facilitative design. I graduated from Simon Fraser University’s Interactive Arts and Technology program with a BSc., where I focused my skills and education in UX/UI and speculative design. Throughout my undergrad, I’ve had opportunities to conduct solo research projects and do co-ops for both public and private sectors, which have allowed me to explore the intersection between design, technology, and social impact. These experiences have shaped my approach as a designer who values empathy, curiosity, and systems thinking—whether I'm designing for near-future scenarios or building intuitive user experiences for urgent real-world applications.

I hope to continue participating in work that combines sustainable, feminist-based design with education to enable inclusive, accessible and enriched learning experiences for people from various backgrounds. As designers, we do more than simply create products or experiences; we shape how people think, interact, and engage with the world. My goal is to use design not just as a tool for problem-solving, but as a means of provoking new ways of thinking and fostering meaningful change. I want to explore how design can be a force for societal transformation, influencing not just what people use, but how they see the world.

During my free time, I enjoy watching video essays and films of any genre, diving into a new niche interest, or decompressing with yoga.
